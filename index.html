Quinoa Salad

A bright and colorful quinoa salad is a great way to continue the meal. I enjoy adding a lot of vegetables to this dish to really provide a wide range of flavors. Sometimes less is more but for this particular salad I found people enjoy the additions since the quinoa itself doesn't have a strong flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 Cups of Organic Red Quinoa
  • 1/2 of Red, Green, Orange and Yellow Organic Bellpepper Each
  • 1/2 Organic English Cucumber
  • 1/2 of an Organic Red Onion
  • 1 Organic Haas Avocado
  • 1/2 Cup of Authentic Greek Feta
  • 1/2 Organic Lemon

Instructions

Begin with the quinoa, and start by using a medium sized pot. For every one cup of quinoa bring two cups of water to a boil. After pouring the quinoa, lower heat to a simmer and leave on until water is fully absorbed and white rings around the outer edges of the grain begin to appear. While the quinoa is cooking, dice vegetables into small bite sized squares. Place quinoa into serving bowl, add diced vegetables, feta, and top off with the squeezed half lemon.

Grilled Salmon and Asparagus

There are many different ways to make an amazing salmon and I dediced to try something I haven't done before for this dish. I've been grilling all kinds of food as of late and figured why not give it a shot with the salmon. Safe to say it turned out, DELICIOUS.

Ingredients

  • 1 Pound of Salmon
  • 12 Oz. Asparagus
  • 2 Teaspons of Seasalt
  • 2 teaspons of Black Pepper
  • 1/2 Organic Lemon

Instructions

Lightly season salmon and asparagus with seasalt and pepper, rememeber it's always easier to add seasoning later as opposed to removing. Follow up by placing slices of the lemon on a sheet of a foil. Then, place the salmon on top of the slices before adding more on top. Finally, wrap the salmon within the foil and place on the grill with asparagus. The heat of the grill should be around 375-400 and cooking time is roughly 15-18 minutes. If you want to check before removing, make a small incision in the foil with a knife to the center of the fish.
